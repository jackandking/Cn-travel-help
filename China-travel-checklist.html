Welcome to Your China Adventure

China offers an incredible diversity of experiences, from ancient historical sites that span thousands of years to ultra-modern cities that define the future. This comprehensive checklist covers the essential destinations and experiences that will give you a deep appreciation for Chinese culture, history, and modern achievements.

How to use this checklist: Check off items as you complete them - your progress is automatically saved in your browser. Each destination includes cultural context to enrich your understanding of what makes these places special.

üìç Beijing - The Imperial Capital
Beijing, China's capital for over 700 years, seamlessly blends imperial grandeur with modern dynamism. Home to some of China's most iconic landmarks and political center of the nation.
üìç Shanghai - The Modern Metropolis
China's financial and commercial hub, Shanghai represents the country's rapid modernization while preserving its colonial heritage along the historic Bund waterfront.
üìç Xi'an - Ancient Capital of Dynasties
The eastern terminus of the Silk Road, Xi'an served as China's capital for over 1,000 years and houses some of the country's most significant archaeological treasures.
üìç Chengdu - Gateway to Tibet and Panda Capital
The laid-back capital of Sichuan Province, famous for its spicy cuisine, tea culture, and being home to the world's largest panda breeding center.
üìç Guilin & Yangshuo - Karst Landscape Wonder
Southern China's most scenic region, featuring the limestone karst landscape that has inspired Chinese painters and poets for centuries.

üí° Essential Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: April-May and September-October offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds
  • Visa Requirements: Most visitors need a Chinese visa - apply well in advance
  • Internet Access: Consider a VPN for accessing blocked websites and services
  • Language: Download translation apps like Pleco or Google Translate with offline Chinese
  • Transportation: High-speed trains connect major cities efficiently - book tickets in advance
  • Cultural Etiquette: Bring business cards, avoid pointing with your finger, and show respect at temples
